Figma prototype 2

Structural Decisions & Mobile-First Logic

In this iteration, I pivoted to a strictly mobile-first design strategy to prioritize structural integrity under high spatial constraints. I replaced the desktop-centric horizontal navigation from Week 1 with a Defined Navigation Model based on a vertical overlay. This is demonstrated across three required mobile frames: a Home page, an open Navigation state, and a detailed Sketches page. My primary structural decision was to utilize a dark utility square as a consistent Interaction Cue for the menu toggle, ensuring that global navigation remains accessible without competing with the primary content hierarchy. By stacking elements vertically, I have optimized the interface for thumb-driven interaction.

Changes from Previous Version

The most significant change is the expansion from a single layout to three distinct mobile-specific page types. I transitioned the navigation from a static header to a collapsed menu state to address the overcrowding issues identified in my initial audit. In the third frame, I introduced a sub-navigation structure for "sketches," demonstrating how the system handles nested information (Sketch 1, 2, and 3) without losing the overall Logical Content Prioritization. This shift ensures that the information architecture remains robust even when the viewport is restricted.

Visible System Status & Hierarchy

To satisfy the Visible System Status requirement, I have used typographic weight and placement to indicate active states. Within the menu overlay, the differentiation between top-level links and sub-level sketches provides immediate orientation. I also established a Clear H1-H4 hierarchy: the primary "Interaction Sketchbook" title acts as the H1 anchor, while the menu items and sub-links represent H2 and H3 levels. This ensures that even in this grayscale, non-styled state, the user can navigate the architecture through scale and proximity alone.

Unresolved Elements

For the desktop expansion, I implemented a centralized structural logic that utilizes the increased horizontal real estate. Unlike the mobile vertical stack, the desktop version clusters nested 'sketch' sub-navigation within a primary content container. This creates a clear focal point for the user’s eye. However, the spatial relationship between the secondary links (Reflections and Figma) and the primary sketch links remains a point of iteration. I need to determine if a grid-based alignment or a split-column layout would better reinforce the information hierarchy as more complex data blocks are introduced.
