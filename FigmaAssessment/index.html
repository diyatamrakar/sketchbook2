Figma Assessment

Navigation Model & System Coherence (20/20)

The navigation logic remains consistent across page types, utilizing viewport-specific adaptations to manage the "Gulf of Execution."

Evidence: Transitioning from a central hub model on mobile, I implemented a dropdown menu for desktop expansion to maintain horizontal header space. For mobile, I utilized a full-screen overlay, ensuring navigation remains accessible without cluttering the primary content hierarchy.

Hierarchy & Mobile-First Logic (14/20)

While mobile-first structural logic was applied, the visual hierarchy and aesthetic execution currently require significant refinement. As there is no clue whether you clicked or not.

Evidence: The desktop layout justifies expansion by utilizing additional width for complex documentation (as seen in frames 18-21). However, the mobile layout suffers from a lack of vertical rhythm and prioritized "State Visibility," making the reading experience less intuitive than the desktop equivalent.

State Visibility & Interaction Cues (15/15)

Interaction cues are predictable across sizes, providing clear feedback loops through component states.

Evidence: I refactored the navigation to include Hover States on desktop to provide immediate feedback. Active states within the mobile overlay and desktop dropdowns ensure the user always understands their location within the system.

Theoretical Integration (15/15)

  • Norman: Pill-shaped buttons and the mobile overlay act as signifiers that reduce the Gulf of Execution.
  • Nielsen: Maintained Consistency and Standards by applying a global header and dropdown system across 20+ frames.
  • Shneiderman: Achieved Closure by organizing the Micro-Project into logical, sequential steps.

Risk & Structural Ambition (5/5)

Effort was prioritized on building a comprehensive, multi-page system rather than isolated frames.

Evidence: Mapping Nielsen Heuristics, Interface Audits, and Golden Rules into a single coherent grayscale template across high content volume demonstrates a commitment to system stability.
